Research Interests

My work in condensed matter physics focuses on quantum magnetism in solid materials. Strongly correlated electrons facilitate the realization of exotic states of matter, characterized by their phase transitions and excitation spectra. My research approaches magnetic materials through parameterized models and a diverse computational toolkit. The difficulty in studying many body systems is overcome by large scale Monte Carlo simulations and cluster expansions to derive estimates of thermodynamic properties. Recent efforts have focused on the high Tc iron-pnictides and rare-earth pyrochlore spin-ices.

Recently, I have become interested in large scale data analysis, machine learning, and data visualization in science. Specifically in the domain of science education, I am seeking to understand how to gauge and improve the effectiveness of education to large audiences.
